Abstract

Velfi is an AI money agent. A user signs in with Google, claims a handle of the form alice.vel, and instructs their money in plain language. "Split rent three ways." "Pay my designer every Friday." "Hold this in escrow until the work ships." Velfi reads the instruction, builds the on-chain workflow, and executes it — with a single confirmation.

The user holds no gas token, writes down no seed phrase, and never sees a wallet address, a swap screen, or the word "blockchain." Underneath, Velfi is a non-custodial Sui wallet. Identity is zkLogin. The handle is a name you own. Payments settle as Move smart contracts with sub-second finality and near-zero fees. The intelligence that turns words into workflows is a language model, retrained on financial logic, that proposes the transaction — but never holds the keys.

This document describes the problem Velfi solves, how the system is built, why it runs on Sui, how it stays secure and non-custodial, how it sustains itself, and what comes next.

Overview

Most money movement follows patterns. Salaries recur. Rent splits the same way every month. Savings should happen automatically but rarely do. Subscriptions, contractor payouts, escrow on deals — these are repeated, rule-based actions. Yet for most people, each one is still a manual task: open an app, re-enter details, confirm, repeat.

Velfi treats a financial instruction as a sentence, not a form. You describe the outcome you want, and the agent assembles the steps. Velfi covers a spectrum of flows from one text:

  • Send — instant transfers to a .vel handle, or to an email address the recipient later claims.
  • Split — one payment divided across many recipients with custom percentage allocations.
  • Stream — continuous payout over time, for salaries and vesting.
  • Schedule — recurring payments on a cadence you set.
  • Escrow — funds held until a condition is met, releasable or cancelable anytime.
  • Automate — rules that trigger on events, like saving a percentage of every inbound payment.

The interface is conversation. The settlement is on-chain. The keys are yours.

The Problem

Two problems sit on top of each other: money management is manual, and the on-chain tools meant to fix that are too hard for normal people to use.

Money management is still manual labor

Even with modern fintech, recurring financial intent requires repeated human effort. Standing orders handle only the simplest cases and break the moment a flow needs logic — a split, a condition, a percentage. People manage money the way they managed it a decade ago: one transaction, one screen, one confirmation at a time.

Crypto made it harder, not easier

Self-custodial finance promised programmable money but shipped a punishing user experience. Seed phrases are a single point of catastrophic failure — lose them and funds are gone forever; leak them and funds are stolen. Gas tokens force users to hold a second asset just to move the first. Wallet addresses are unreadable strings that invite costly mistakes. Industry research has repeatedly identified key management and transaction complexity as the central barriers to mainstream self-custody adoption. [1] [2]

AI advises but does not act

The current wave of "AI finance" tools summarize, categorize, and recommend — but they stop at advice. They cannot safely execute, because handing an autonomous model custody of funds is dangerous, and because most chains have no clean way for an agent to propose a transaction that a human signs without surrendering control. The gap between knowing what to do with your money and having it done remains unbridged.

The gap

People want to instruct money in plain language and have it execute safely. Manual fintech can't automate the logic. Self-custody is too hard to use. AI tools can't act. Velfi closes all three at once.

The Solution

Velfi is an agent that sits between human intent and on-chain settlement. The flow is four steps, and only the first time involves any setup at all.

  1. Sign in with Google. zkLogin derives a self-custodial Sui wallet from the Google account through a zero-knowledge proof. No seed phrase exists to lose or leak. Switch devices, sign in again, and the same wallet returns.
  2. Claim a .vel name. A human-readable handle replaces wallet addresses — for the user and for anyone paying them.
  3. Tell Velfi what you want. The instruction is written in plain English. The agent interprets it and builds the corresponding on-chain workflow.
  4. Confirm once. The user approves with a single tap. Velfi executes, and for recurring flows, keeps executing automatically.

How an instruction becomes a transaction

When a user writes "stream $4,000 to alex.vel over 30 days," the agent parses intent, resolves the handle, selects the correct payment primitive, fills the parameters, and presents a structured transaction for the user to sign. The model proposes; the user disposes. Funds never move without an explicit, key-backed signature from the account owner.

Send $50 to jordan@gmail.com — they claim it when they sign up.

An email recipient who isn't yet a user receives a claimable transfer. They sign in, claim a handle, and the funds land — turning every payment into an invitation.

Why We Built on Sui

An agent that executes money on a user's behalf needs a settlement layer that is fast, cheap, safe to program, and able to abstract its own complexity away. Sui was built for exactly this.

Sub-second finality

Simple payments on Sui confirm in well under a second through its consensus design. [3] For a conversational money agent, instant settlement is the difference between "it's done" and "please wait." The interaction has to feel like sending a message, not submitting a request.

Near-zero fees and gaslessness

Sui's low fees and support for sponsored transactions let Velfi remove the gas token from the user's mental model entirely. The user never holds, sees, or thinks about a fee asset — the chain becomes invisible plumbing.

The Move language

Velfi's payment primitives are written in Move, a language designed for safe asset handling, where digital assets are first-class resources that cannot be accidentally copied or destroyed. [4] For software that moves real money on a schedule and without per-action human review, this safety model is foundational.

zkLogin and object storage, natively

zkLogin is a native Sui primitive: it lets a user authenticate with an existing Web2 identity provider and derive a wallet from a zero-knowledge proof, with no seed phrase. [5] Payment receipts and workflow metadata are stored on Walrus, Sui's decentralized storage layer, keeping records verifiable without a central database.

Security

Velfi's security model rests on a single principle: the agent proposes, the user disposes, and only the user holds the keys.

Non-custodial by construction

Velfi never takes custody of funds. Every transaction is signed by the user's own key, derived via zkLogin. The agent can assemble a transaction but cannot move money without an explicit signature from the account owner. There is no admin key that can sweep balances.

No seed phrase to compromise

Because identity is derived from a zero-knowledge proof over an existing Google login rather than a stored secret phrase, the classic attack surface — phishing a seed phrase, finding a written-down backup, draining via a leaked key — is removed at the design level. [5]

Safe-by-default contract logic

Payment primitives are implemented in Move, whose resource model prevents whole classes of asset bugs — double-spends, accidental loss, reentrancy patterns — by construction. [4] Recurring and conditional flows run as deterministic contract logic, not as an autonomous model with spending power.

The AI cannot spend

The language model's role is strictly interpretation and proposal. It converts words into a structured transaction for human approval. It has no key, no signing authority, and no path to move funds on its own. A compromised or mistaken model cannot drain a user — the worst case is a proposed transaction the user simply rejects.

On-chain and verifiable

Every executed flow is a transaction on Sui, and receipts are recorded on Walrus. Nothing about a user's payment history depends on trusting Velfi's servers — it is independently verifiable on-chain.

How Velfi Earns

Velfi has no token. Its economics are built on usage, not speculation, so incentives stay aligned with users actually moving money.

Transaction margin

Sending value between .vel handles is effectively free. Velfi earns a small, transparent spread only where real-world value conversion happens — when money enters or leaves the system, or crosses currencies — comparable to how modern remittance products price, only thinner because settlement runs on Sui rather than a chain of correspondent banks.

Premium automation

Advanced and high-volume workflows — business payroll, treasury-style scheduled flows, larger escrow arrangements — are offered under a premium tier for teams and power users, while everyday personal use stays free.

Handle and namespace value

The .vel namespace is a durable asset. Premium and reserved handles create a sustainable, non-extractive revenue line that grows with the network rather than taxing ordinary payments.

Principle

Velfi makes money when users successfully move money. There is no token to inflate, no yield to manufacture, and no incentive misaligned with the product simply working.

Roadmap

Velfi ships in stages, each one widening what a single instruction can do.

Now — Foundation

  • zkLogin authentication and self-custodial wallet derivation.
  • The .vel handle system.
  • Core payment primitives in Move: send, split, stream, schedule, escrow.
  • The AI agent that turns plain-language instructions into proposed transactions.
  • Email-to-claim transfers that onboard new users through payments.

Next — Intelligence and reach

  • A financial-domain model, retrained on payment logic, for more accurate and nuanced instruction handling.
  • Conditional and trigger-based automation expanded to richer rule sets.
  • A beneficiary and continuity layer for shared and delegated flows.
  • Mobile app release.

Later — Platform

  • A developer SDK so other payment apps can integrate Velfi's primitives and agent directly.
  • Broader currency support and additional on/off-ramp partners.
  • Team and treasury tooling for businesses.

Conclusion

For most of history, instructing money meant doing the work yourself, one action at a time. Programmable money promised to change that, then buried the promise under seed phrases, gas tokens, and addresses no human should have to read.

Velfi removes the work and the friction at the same time. You say what you want in a sentence; the agent builds the workflow; the chain settles it in under a second; and the keys never leave your hands. Sign in with Google, claim a name, and tell your money what to do.

The interface is conversation. The settlement is on-chain. The control is yours. That is the whole idea — and everything on this roadmap is in service of making that one sentence true for more people, in more places, doing more things with their money than they ever could by hand.
